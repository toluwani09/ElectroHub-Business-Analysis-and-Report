📊 ElectroHub Business Intelligence & Sales Analysis
🧠 Project Overview

This project presents an end-to-end Business Intelligence analysis of ElectroHub’s sales data, with the goal of uncovering actionable insights to support strategic decision-making.

While initial performance metrics suggested strong revenue generation, deeper analysis revealed underlying structural issues affecting long-term business sustainability.

🎯 Business Objectives

The analysis was designed to address key business questions:

What are the top and bottom performing products by sales, profit, and quantity?
How have sales trends evolved over time?
What is the relationship between sales and profit?
How does performance compare across different time periods?
How effective are discount and promotion strategies?
What trends exist in customer purchasing behavior (orders)?
How do sales vary across different locations?
🗂️ Dataset & Preparation

The project utilized raw transactional and customer data, which was:

Cleaned to handle missing values and inconsistencies
Transformed for analytical readiness
Enhanced through feature engineering
Structured into a scalable data model

💡 Business Recommendations
✅ Product Strategy

Top Performers:<br>
Laptop Pro 15 and Smartphone X200 account for 38% of total revenue over the past 6 months.<br>
Recommendation: Maintain stock levels for these high-demand products and explore complementary accessories (e.g., laptop bags, phone cases) to increase average basket size by 5–7%.<br><br>

Low Performers:<br>
Wireless Mouse Basic, USB-C Hub, and Tablet Cover Standard collectively contribute <2% of revenue each and have >30% unsold inventory rate.<br>
Recommendation: Consider phasing out or bundling these with higher-performing products to improve turnover and reduce storage costs.<br><br>

✅ Pricing & Revenue Mix

Pricing Insights:<br>
Smartphone X200 generates 15% profit margin at current pricing; Laptop Pro 15 is 28% margin.<br>
Recommendation: Introduce tiered pricing or bundle offers (e.g., Smartphone X200 + Earbuds) to increase revenue per transaction by approx. ₦5,000–₦7,000.<br><br>

Revenue Diversification:<br>
Currently, top 5 products contribute 55% of revenue, leaving middle-tier products underutilized.<br>
Recommendation: Promote mid-tier products (Laptop Pro 13, Smartphone Z50) via targeted campaigns to balance revenue mix.<br><br>

✅ Promotion Optimization

Promotion Performance:<br>
Holiday Discount 10% and Buy 1 Get 1 Free (USB-C Hub) accounted for 70% of sales uplift, but the ROI for the USB-C Hub promotion was -8% due to low sell-through.<br>
Recommendation: Limit extreme discounts to high-demand products; standardize discount range to max 15%.<br>
Recommendation: Use ROI ≥ 20% as the baseline for future campaigns, tracked per product category.<br><br>

✅ Customer Retention

Customer Insights:<br>
Top 20% of customers (based on purchase frequency) account for 62% of revenue, while 35% of customers made only 1 purchase in the last 6 months.<br>
Recommendation: Launch loyalty programs targeting repeat customers (e.g., 5% off on second purchase of Laptop Pro or Smartphone X200) to increase repeat purchase rate by 10–12% over the next quarter.<br><br>

Segmentation:<br>
Segment customers by product preference:<br>

Electronics Enthusiasts → mostly laptops & phones<br>
Accessory Buyers → mostly chargers, hubs, covers<br>
Recommendation: Tailor marketing campaigns to each segment for better engagement.<br><br>
